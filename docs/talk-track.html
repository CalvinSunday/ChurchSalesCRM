Curiosity first • Short • Respectful

Call flow (90 seconds to earn the next step)

StepWhat you do
1Permission: “Did I catch you at a bad time?”
2Context + proof: “We’ve helped a few churches around Redding/Shasta with Sunday audio + livestream.”
3Two questions: Ask, then stop talking.
4Summarize: Repeat what you heard in one sentence.
5Next step: Send sample + schedule 15–20 min assessment/demo setup call.

If they ask “What do you do?” (non-salesy)

“We help churches make Sunday audio easier to understand — especially speech and the livestream — usually by tightening the mix and coaching the team. But first I’m curious what you’re dealing with.”

Scripts

Gatekeeper (front desk/admin)
Open: “Hi — quick question. I’m trying to reach whoever owns Sunday audio or livestream.”
Early proof: “We’ve helped churches around Redding/Shasta like Cottonwood Assembly of God, Redding Christian Fellowship, and Palo Cedro Seventh-day Adventist.”
Ask: “Who should I speak with about that at your church?”
If ‘what is this about?’ “Not a sales pitch — I’m just trying to understand if you’re having audio/livestream pain points. If it’s not relevant, I’ll close the loop.”
If they’re busy: “No problem — when’s the best time to catch [Name] for 2 minutes?”
Micro-close “Can you connect me, or share the best direct line/time?”
Decision-maker (Worship Pastor)
Open (first name only): “Hi Pastor [Name] — this is [FirstName]. Did I catch you at a bad time?”
Context + proof (early): “I work with a few churches around Redding/Shasta on Sunday audio and livestream — Cottonwood Assembly of God, Redding Christian Fellowship, and Palo Cedro Seventh-day Adventist.”
Frame (no pricing): “I’m not calling to talk money today. I’m trying to understand whether audio or livestream is a headache for you.”
Two questions:
1) “If a first-time visitor watched your stream, could they understand every word?”
2) “If you could fix one thing about your audio or stream, what would you change?”
Summary: “So what I’m hearing is [repeat their pain in 10 words]. Did I get that right?”
Next step: “If you’re open to it, I’ll send a quick sample page (2–3 minutes). If your board can do multitrack, we can also do a free remote mix demo. Should I send the sample first, or do you want to pick a quick time for the next step?”

Discovery (ask, then shut up)

Frame: “I’ll ask a few questions, then I’ll summarize what I heard — and you tell me if I’m off.”
  • Are you streaming every week, most weeks, or not currently?
  • Do you hear more complaints about in-room sound, or the livestream?
  • Who mixes most weeks — staff, volunteer rotation, or a mix?
  • Do you have a dedicated broadcast mix, or is it a feed from FOH?
  • How consistent does it feel week to week (1–10)?
  • What console are you using right now (digital or analog)?
  • Can your mixer do multitrack (individual channels), or only a stereo record?
  • If we fixed one thing in 30 days, what would matter most?

3-question loop (after your summary)

1) “Is this making sense so far?”
2) “Are you 100% comfortable with this so far?”
3) “Where do we go from here?”

Use 1 stat (then back to questions)

Pushpay’s 2024 State of Church Tech report: 91% of churches livestream worship services; 90% offer a hybrid model.

Objections (keep it curious)

Common objection responses
  • “Send info.” → “Totally. To make it relevant — are you more concerned about speech clarity, feedback, or the livestream mix?”
  • “We’re good.” → “Love that. What would have to be true for you to say ‘we should improve this’ — clarity, consistency, or volunteer stress?”
  • “No budget.” → “Understood — no pricing today. Is it still worth a quick assessment (and a free demo if you can multitrack) so you know your options?”
  • “We already have someone.” → “Great. How are you measuring success — fewer complaints, more consistency, or a better stream experience?”
  • “Not a priority.” → “Fair. What is the priority — and is audio/livestream affecting it (growth, visitors, volunteer burnout)?”

Analog branch (still move the deal)

“That’s helpful — analog can still sound great, but it usually takes more intention. The free remote mix demo needs multitrack, so we can’t do that part yet — but we can still do a short assessment and map a step-by-step path.”

Pastor [Name] — thanks for the quick chat. Here’s the sample page: [LINK]. If your mixer can do multitrack, next step is the free remote mix demo + a 15–20 minute assessment call. Does [two options] work this week?

Sales guardrails

  • Do not promise “100% refund guaranteed” unless it is a written policy you will honor.
  • Safer line: “We stand behind our work, and any guarantees are in writing in the agreement.”
  • If they ask for pricing: “Happy to cover that on the next call once we understand your situation.”

FREE Remote Mix Demo: board quick check

Requirement: multitrack WAV files (USB/SD) OR a laptop connected via USB audio interface.

Common boards (built-in multitrack to USB/SD)
  • Soundcraft Ui24R — multitrack direct to USB drive
  • Allen & Heath Qu series — Qu-Drive multitrack to USB drive
  • Allen & Heath SQ series — SQ-Drive multitrack to USB drive
  • PreSonus StudioLive Series III — multitrack to SD card
  • QSC TouchMix — multitrack to USB drive
  • Zoom LiveTrak L-20 (and similar) — discrete channels to SD
Common boards (multitrack via USB to a laptop)
  • Behringer X32 / Midas M32 — multitrack via USB interface (X-USB)
  • Behringer X Air (XR18/XR16) — multitrack via USB to laptop
  • Behringer WING — multitrack via USB
  • Yamaha TF series — multitrack via USB-to-host
  • Mackie DL32R/DL32S — multitrack via USB (varies by setup)
What reps ask (simple)

1) “Can your mixer record individual channels (multitrack) — not just a stereo WAV?”
2) “Do you record to USB/SD in the mixer, or to a laptop?”
3) “If yes, we’ll send a 1-page checklist and a link to upload the WAV files.”
